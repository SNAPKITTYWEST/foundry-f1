# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

This repository exists to build exact, auditable systems. The standard here is
serious technical conduct.

## Expected Conduct

- discuss the code, math, tests, and evidence directly
- make claims that you can defend
- preserve provenance when reporting results
- respect licensing, gated surfaces, and contribution boundaries
- report defects precisely enough that someone else can reproduce them

## Unacceptable Conduct

- harassment, threats, or personal abuse
- deliberate misrepresentation of proofs, receipts, benchmarks, or authorship
- attempts to bypass licensing or gated execution surfaces and present that as
  legitimate contribution
- intentionally introducing malware, exfiltration, or nondeterministic behavior
- publishing private capability material, secrets, or local operator data

## Enforcement

Maintainers may reject patches, remove access, or archive threads when conduct
or contributions undermine auditability, safety, or licensing boundaries.

## Reporting

Report conduct or integrity issues through the maintainer channel already used
for this repo. Include the smallest reproducible context, affected files, and
any relevant receipts.
